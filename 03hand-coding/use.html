Uses of Stop Motion

The history of stop motion dates back over one hundred years, before film itself even. Stop motion is a film technique where a physical object is moved and photographed at every step. When each frame is played in quick succession, the objects appear to move on their own. This animation technique is used for both entertainment and commercial purposes. When getting started there are many aspects that aspiring animators and filmmakers need to consider.

Types of Stop-Motion

  • Object-Motion
  • Claymation
  • Pixilation Stop Motion
  • Cutout Animation
  • Puppet Animation
  • Silhouette Stop Motion

Considerations When Using Stop-Motion

  1. Setting
  2. Camera Angle
  3. Remote Triggers or Timers
  4. Manual Settings
  5. Lighting
  6. Frame Rate
  7. Small Incremental Movements
  8. Audio
